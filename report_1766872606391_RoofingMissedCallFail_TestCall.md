## 2) What To Fix First (Top 3)
- Issue: Opener is weak and asks for permission ("Can I take 20 seconds?"), inviting an instant "No."
  Fix rule: State your purpose and their potential problem immediately without asking for time.
  Better line: "Hey Trent, it's [Your Name] - the reason for the call is we help Apex Roofing capture the emergency leak calls you're missing after 5pm."

- Issue: No rapport built; jumped straight into a generic pitch against a wall of resistance.
  Fix rule: Anchor your reason to a specific, high-stakes scenario they know is painful.
  Better line: "When a storm hits or someone calls with a leak after hours, that lead usually calls two more roofers before you even see the voicemail."

- Issue: Failed to control the frame; kept talking after clear "stop" signals, damaging any future contact.
  Fix rule: When you get a hard "stop," respect it immediately and reframe to a low-friction exit.
  Better line: "Understood, I'll note that. Last thing: if you ever do have a problem with missed calls costing jobs, the fix is one text away. Have a good one."

## 3) Best Objection Responses (word-for-word)
1. **To "I'm not buying anything / Stop calling":** "Totally fair, Trent - this isn't a sales call right now. It's just a quick way to stop losing the leak calls you're already missing. Can I shoot you a single text to show you what it looks like?"
2. **To "We're busy":** "That's exactly why I called. When you're swamped, calls get missed and that's money walking to your competitors. This just auto-texts those callers so you still get the lead."
3. **To "I don't care":** "Most roofers don't, until they realize how many [Industry] jobs they're losing to the next guy who answers. It takes 30 seconds to see if it's for you. Can I text you the example?"
